In contrast, white fiber is extracted from younger coconuts and is finer and softer, ideal for upholstery stuffing, brushes, and biodegradable products. Both types cater to diverse industries, including agriculture, construction, and textiles, contributing to the growing demand for natural and sustainable materials.</p></p>
