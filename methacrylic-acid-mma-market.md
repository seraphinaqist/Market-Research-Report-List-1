<p><p>Methacrylic Acid (MMA) is predominantly categorized based on its production routes, including ACH (Acetone Cyanohydrin), Isobutylene, Ethylene, and others. ACH involves the reaction of acetone with hydrogen cyanide, producing MMA with high efficiency. Isobutylene is derived from the petrochemical industry, while Ethylene is another fundamental building block for various chemical processes. The "Others" category encompasses alternative methods and lesser-used pathways for MMA synthesis. Each method influences the market's dynamics, pricing, and availability based on raw material accessibility and production cost.</p></p>
