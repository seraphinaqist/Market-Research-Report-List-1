<p><p>The trifocal intraocular lens (IOL) market is primarily divided into hydrophobic and hydrophilic acrylic IOLs. Hydrophobic acrylic IOLs are designed to repel water, enhancing resistance to glare and increasing biocompatibility, making them ideal for patients with a higher risk of complications. In contrast, hydrophilic acrylic IOLs absorb water, offering improved stability and ease of handling during implantation. Both types aim to provide multifocal vision correction, allowing patients to see clearly at various distances, thereby improving post-operative quality of life.</p></p>
